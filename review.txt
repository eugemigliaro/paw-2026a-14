1. Verification & password-reset routes broken for authenticated users (403)

webapp/.../config/SecurityConfig.java:50 — High

/login (GET), /register, /register/resend-verification, /forgot-password, /password-reset/**, and /verifications/** were changed from .permitAll() to .anonymous(). In Spring Security, .anonymous() grants access only to an AnonymousAuthenticationToken; an authenticated session is denied with 403. So a logged-in user who clicks a password-reset link, a resend-verification link, or any /verifications/** link gets a 403 before the controller (and its VerificationExceptionHandler/PasswordResetExceptionHandler) ever runs. AGENTS.md explicitly requires preserving access through verification links, and no test exercises the authenticated-hits-these-routes path. /login and /register GET being anonymous-only is defensible; the email-driven recovery routes are not.
